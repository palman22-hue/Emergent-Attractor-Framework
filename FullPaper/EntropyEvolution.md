# Entropy Evolution  
**Qualitative Dynamics of Entropy in Voluntary Alignment and Forced Uniformity**

This document provides a conceptual explanation of how system entropy evolves over time in the Emergent Attractor Framework. It summarizes the qualitative behavior observed in RUN#C1 (voluntary alignment) and RUN#C2 (forced uniformity), supported by ASCII diagrams.

---

## 1. Introduction

Entropy serves as a measure of uncertainty within each agent and across the system as a whole. Tracking entropy over time reveals how different alignment mechanisms shape system stability, coherence, and adaptability.

Two distinct patterns emerge:

- **Voluntary Alignment (RUN#C1):**  
  Entropy decreases gradually and stabilizes at a moderate, coherent plateau.

- **Forced Uniformity (RUN#C2):**  
  Entropy decreases rapidly and converges to a low, compressed state.

These patterns reflect the structural differences between emergent attractor basins and compressed uniform states.

The entropy curves support the theorem:

- **Voluntary alignment** → entropy reduction + resilience

Entropy evolution provides a clear diagnostic tool for distinguishing emergent coherence from mechanical compression. RUN#C1 and RUN#C2 demonstrate that:

- Both reduce entropy

This distinction is central to understanding alignment in complex systems.
